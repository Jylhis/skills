# German CEFR syllabus — what to teach at each level

A topic map for pitching i+1. Don't introduce a band's grammar until the prior
band's core is reliable in production (not just recognition).

## A1 — Breakthrough
- Greetings, introductions, numbers, dates, time.
- Present tense (regular + sein/haben); common stem-changing verbs.
- **Nominative and Accusative** cases; definite/indefinite articles.
- Gender + plural learned per noun; basic vocabulary (family, food, home).
- Yes/no and W-questions; V2 word order; `kein` and `nicht` negation.
- Goal: simple sentences about self and immediate needs.

## A2 — Waystage
- **Dative case**; dative verbs (helfen, danken, gefallen); dative prepositions.
- **Separable verbs**; modal verbs.
- **Perfekt** tense (haben/sein + participle) for past events.
- Two-way prepositions (Wechselpräpositionen) with accusative/dative contrast.
- Comparatives/superlatives; possessive articles.
- Begin Swiss German listening: greetings, numbers, everyday dialect words
  (flagged as spoken).
- Goal: routine exchanges, simple past narration.

## B1 — Threshold
- **Genitive case**; genitive prepositions (wegen, trotz, während).
- Subordinate clauses (weil, dass, wenn, obwohl) with verb-final order.
- **Präteritum** for narration; Plusquamperfekt.
- Relative clauses; reflexive verbs; adjective endings (weak/strong/mixed).
- **Konjunktiv II** for politeness and hypotheticals (würde/hätte/wäre).
- Goal: connected text on familiar topics; express opinions and plans.

## B2 — Vantage
- Passive voice (werden-passive, sein-passive); Passiversatz.
- Konjunktiv I for reported speech.
- Nominalization and verb-noun collocations (Funktionsverbgefüge).
- Connectors and discourse structure; n-declension nouns.
- Goal: clear, detailed text; argue a viewpoint; understand most native media.

## C1 — Effective Operational Proficiency
- Advanced adjective/participle constructions (Partizipialattribute).
- Subjunctive nuance, modal particles (doch, mal, ja, eben) for register.
- Idioms, collocations, register shifting; complex Genitive/Partizip styles.
- Goal: fluent, flexible language for social, academic, professional use.

## C2 — Mastery
- Stylistic precision, near-native idiomatic and cultural nuance.
- Effortless comprehension of dialect-coloured speech, including Swiss German.
- Goal: understand virtually everything; summarize and argue spontaneously.

## Sequencing tips
- Cases are the spine: lock Nominative/Accusative (A1) before Dative (A2) before
  Genitive (B1). Interleave them once each is introduced.
- Drill gender from day one — late gender gaps are expensive to fix.
- Move from Perfekt (spoken past) to Präteritum (written past) at A2→B1.
