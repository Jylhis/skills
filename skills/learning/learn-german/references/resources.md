# German comprehensible-input and vocabulary resources

Pointers for generating level-appropriate input and prioritizing vocabulary.
Confirm URLs at use time; treat counts as approximate.

## Frequency-first vocabulary
- The most frequent ~1,000 German words cover the large majority of everyday
  text; ~2,000–3,000 gives comfortable A2–B1 reading. Teach high-frequency words
  before rare ones.
- Sources: a "Goethe-Zertifikat" wordlist per level (A1/A2/B1 official lists),
  the Leipzig Corpora frequency lists, and the DWDS frequency data.
- **Agent move:** when generating example sentences, keep ~95–98% of words inside
  the learner's known band; introduce at most 1–2 new words per sentence, glossed.

## Graded readers and listening
- **Nachrichtenleicht** and **DW "Nachrichten in einfacher Sprache"** — slow,
  simplified German news (good A2–B1 input).
- **Deutsche Welle (DW) Learn German** — free courses, "Nicos Weg" (A1–B1 video
  story), graded articles.
- **Easy German** (YouTube) — street interviews with dual subtitles; includes
  Swiss episodes for dialect exposure.
- Graded readers: André Klein's "Dino lernt Deutsch" series; Olly Richards'
  "Short Stories in German".

## Grammar references (for the learner)
- **Deutsche Grammatik 2.0**, **Lingolia German**, and the Goethe-Institut
  materials for level-by-level grammar.

## How to use these in a session
- Pull or generate a short text at the learner's level; pre-teach 3–5 key words;
  read for meaning first, then mine 2–4 cloze cards from it.
- Prefer authentic-but-simplified input over isolated word lists — context drives
  acquisition (comprehensible input).
- For Swiss exposure, use Easy German's Swiss episodes or local content, but keep
  any written practice in Hochdeutsch.
